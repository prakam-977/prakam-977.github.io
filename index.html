Intro

I am Prakam, a software developer and researcher focused on the intersection of low-latency systems, market connectivity infrastructure, and AI reasoning. My background is in building performance-critical C++ systems for exchange connectivity, order routing, and real-time market data processing, dealing directly with issues like race conditions, tail-latency spikes, and distributed correctness.

I am currently pursuing full-time research on reasoning capabilities of large language models with Prof. Carolyn Rosé and Prof. David R. Mortensen at Carnegie Mellon University, studying systematic evaluation of multi-step reasoning and test-time scaling strategies.

I am a contributor to PBEBench, a synthetic and contamination-free benchmark for evaluating multi-step inductive reasoning through string rewrite tasks, inspired by program synthesis and historical linguistics. My work focused on experimental design, benchmark construction, evaluation methodology, and analysis of reasoning generalization.

My long-term research interests include:

  • Reasoning evaluation and controllability of LLMs
  • Test-time scaling vs. long-chain reasoning tradeoffs
  • Neuro-symbolic and program-synthesis-based benchmarks
  • Low-latency infrastructure for real-time AI systems
