Intro

I am Prakam, an AI researcher (May 2025 – Present) focused on the systematic evaluation of reasoning capabilities of large language models. I collaborate with Prof. Carolyn Rosé and Prof. David R. Mortensen at Carnegie Mellon University, studying multi-step reasoning benchmarks and test-time inference behaviors.

I am a contributor to PBEBench, a synthetic and contamination-free benchmark for evaluating multi-step inductive reasoning through string rewrite tasks inspired by program synthesis and historical linguistics. My work spans experimental design, benchmark construction, evaluation methodology, and analysis of reasoning generalization.

Prior to research, I worked as a low-latency systems engineer in high-frequency trading and market connectivity (2022 – 2025), building performance-critical C++ systems for exchange gateways, order routing, and real-time market data processing. This involved hands-on experience with race conditions, tail-latency spikes, kernel/network tuning, and distributed correctness issues under microsecond-scale latency constraints.

My long-term research interests include:

  • Reasoning evaluation and controllability of LLMs
  • Neuro-symbolic and program-synthesis-based benchmarks
  • Low-latency infrastructure for real-time AI systems
